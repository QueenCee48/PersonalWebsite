A commission piece for a friend of a friend. I did two versions, a light angel and a dark angel, but I think this one was my favorite. May 27, 2024

I wanted to try the grayscale challenge I keep seeing on Instagram and TikTok, but I wanted to try drawing someone free-hand. Why not be my own muse? Plus I haven't drawn a self portrait in a couple years. Basically, I chose a color pallette which consisted of mostly blues, greens and purples. Then I put my iPad in grayscale which made literally everything on the screen appear in grayscale. Then I shuffled the pallette around so I didn't know where each color was. Then you have to rely on matching the values in grayscale to the reference photo (which also appeared in grayscale). When I finished, I was actually pleased with how it came out...but it was mainly green and blue which I didn't feel fit the vibe it was giving. I ended up adjusting the hue, saturation, brightness, and curves to make it more blue. Seems like I'll have to keep making a self portrait every 2 years.

November 22, 2022

Another self portrait 2 years later. I wanted to try a new coloring technique I saw someone do on Instagram or YouTube...I can't remember. But anywaysss, it is a much longer method than I am used to, but the results are pretty smooth. Basically, I traced my picture for the outline and then I color-picked a spot on my photo and colored a small circle in that same spot on the coloring layer. After the whole picture is filled with a ton of colored circles, I blended them together with a smudge brush. That is how I colored the whole thing. Waaay too much work. Ask me if it was worth it...

January 7, 2022

This is an original character I made in April of 2020. I used an app called Gemsona Maker to create a character in the Steven Universe style. Then I found a reference picture on Pinterest for the pose I wanted. Once I had those set, I drew the character free-hand in my style at the time (which really was non-existent). I was trying exercises to develop an art style. This piece to the right, is the updated version of the character after developing my skills for some years. This time around, I found a base pose sketch for the body and then re-drew the character based on the pose. I wanted to focus on the stylization of the character than spend too much time trying to make up and draw a pose. I definitely like this version better. She does not have a name though.
